About Me
As a young entrepreneur, I am passionate about contributing to innovation and progress in an ever-increasing complex and ambiguous world. My desire to constantly learn and broaden my mind is fostered across all areas of life; especially quantum- and astrophysics, artificial intelligence, sustainability, economics, entrepreneurship, health, history, and sports.

I want to work at the cutting edge of progress, creating real value and helping civilization to thrive and prosper. The guiding value to me is sustainable happiness, a truly better world, which is much closer than most people think. Technology itself can never solve all the problems, as it always creates good and bad use cases at the same time, therefore it is about us and how we apply the tools we develop.
At the center of each innovation is an idea and the process of developing, validating and adapting the idea to solve real problems fascinates me. My goal is to enable as many of these ideas as possible to reach the point where they can disrupt the current status quo.

Combining my mechanical engineering background and my experience in business development with profound coding skills enables me to fully deep dive into problems and their inherent solution space. My added value lays in identifying, evaluating and steering innovation processes to increase the probability of success. Combining that with a valuable network and access to financial ressources you end at the high-level job description of an angel investor.
My path already taught me that thorough planning is not possible as the best opportunities come along the way. That said my place of living is constantly changing as one part of my mission is to explore and learn. This way I learned how lucky and priviledged I am, but that true happiness nonetheless comes from within and is detached from the material world.

Let's play positive-sum games where the cake gets bigger instead of distributed. We got this one shot and really nothing to lose, so I want to make the best of it and see what's possible.

Marlo Kürner
